Data Explorer — Genie Space

Genie Space is a natural-language data exploration interface powered by Databricks AI. Instead of writing SQL, you can ask questions in plain English — like "Which businesses in postcode 2450 are high risk?" or "Show me suppliers exposed to flooding" — and receive instant, accurate answers drawn directly from the EcoResilience data platform.

The Genie Space connects to curated views of business risk scores, supply chain exposure, real-time weather observations, and active transport hazards across NSW. All data is governed through Unity Catalog with full lineage and access controls.

Who benefits from Genie Space?

  • • Emergency Coordinators — quickly identify which regions and businesses face imminent disaster risk without needing technical SQL knowledge.
  • • Business Owners & Operators — explore their own supply chain exposure and understand which suppliers may be disrupted by weather events.
  • • Government & Policy Analysts — aggregate risk data across postcodes and entity types to inform disaster funding allocation and recovery planning.
  • • Insurance & Financial Services — assess portfolio-level disaster exposure by querying business risk and weather data conversationally.
  • • Data & Analytics Teams — prototype queries and validate data quality through a low-friction, no-code interface before building dashboards.

First-Time Access Setup

To interact with the EcoResilience Genie Space, you must be a member of the eco_resilience_genie_users group in Databricks.

  1. Log in to your Databricks workspace.
  2. Click your profile icon (top-right) → Settings → Identity and access → Groups.
  3. Search for eco_resilience_genie_users and click Request access.
  4. If no self-service option is available, email admin@ecoresilience.ai to request group membership.
  5. Once approved, return here and click Open Genie Space above.

What this grants you: CAN VIEW permission on the Genie Space plus SELECT access to the underlying views in eco_resilience.gold (business risk, supply chain, weather, and hazards data). You do not need separate SQL warehouse permissions — the space uses embedded compute credentials.
